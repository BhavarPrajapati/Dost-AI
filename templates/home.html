Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519), born in Vinci, Italy,was a Renaissance artist, inventor

and scientist.Famous for Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, he also designed flying

machines and studied anatomy. Known for endless curiosity, he worked

across Italy and France, leaving a timeless legacy of art and innovation.

Thomas Dambo

Thomas Dambo is a Danish “recycle artist” famous for creating large sculptures—especially

trolls—from reclaimed and recycled materials. His art merges folklore, environmental

awareness, and community participation. Dambo builds hidden installations around

the world that invite people to explore nature, rethink waste, and find beauty in

what others discard.

Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi (Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi), leader of India’s freedom struggle,

promoted khadi and “Made in India” clothes through the Swadeshi movement. He

urged people to boycott British textiles and spin their own cloth. His principles

of nonviolence, truth, and simplicity inspired millions, shaping India’s independence

and leaving a global legacy.
